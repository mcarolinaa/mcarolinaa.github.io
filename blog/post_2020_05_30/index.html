Tools for (collaborate) and learn

(All posts in this blog may not reflect true scientific value, since they are merely reflecting my opinions and views on certain matters at the time it was written.)



A little while ago I discovered Wikibooks and I found the initiative very interesting.

In summary, Wikibooks is a collaborative project for authoring and editing books. An ‘Important sister project’ from Wikipedia, as described on the page.

Wikibooks is one of the many initiatives from Wikimedia project. Some of other projects include Wikiversity, Wikimedia Labs, Wikinews, Wikidata and so on… (I confess that I did not know about most of them).



Some points that have caught my attention so far about Wikibooks:

+ Type of material

Wikibooks deals with a specific type of material, what they call ‘textbook’, but which actually refers to textbooks, manuals and instructional texts.

The term ‘books’ in Wikibooks can give an unreal impression about the platform’s proposal; it is not meant for any type of book.



+ Everyone can be an author

Anyone can edit the books, which are in different stages of completion. Anyone can contribute in any way to the development of the materials.

This also has to do with the fact that any material added to the platform must be editable.

In a way, this resembles open-source development, in its specific way.



+ Who makes things work?

The whole community works on a voluntary basis. Everything can be done anonymously, and things work out due to the voluntary efforts of the community. That seems pretty incredible to me.



+ Do I need to be a ‘writer’ to use the platform?

You can be a writer, an editor, or simply a reader.

But you must remember that it is not a platform that only contains books of all types and areas.



+ Finding materials

Being a reader, an easy way for you to find materials that interest you, is by using the shelves. There you can find materials considering some main areas, such as:

+ Computing

+ Humanities

+ Science

+ Mathematics

+ Languages

+ Engineering

and many others!

In each section you will find several works, in the most varied construction stages - a detail indicated at the beginning of each subject’s subsection.



+ There are materials with recognized superior quality

A very interesting section to search is Featured Books. Here, the community indicates which are the best available materials - and which should also serve as inspiration for people to improve the quality of their books!



My intention, at least for the moment, is not to be an author. I was interested in the page because of the availability of materials from areas that I’m interested in, such as technology, programming and languages. And precisely because I have been looking for teaching and instruction materials.

It is also a good choice of material you can use for preparing classes. You can make adaptations to the extent that you deem necessary (I imagine this in a context of dealing with a subject in which you already have a good level of knowledge), and this may eventually lead to a contribution to the platform.

It also seemed interesting to me the possibility of translating materials (or pieces of it) in order to train the activity and make your portfolio, but not to make available on the platform (the idea here is a more personal project).

In fact, I got to know the platform due to this specific possibility, while I was looking for suggestions of technical materials that I could edit (translate) without having problems with licensing and things like that.

Of course, for that I must follow the rules for correct assignment of works. More license and attribution details can be seen here:

https://en.wikibooks.org/wiki/Wikibooks:Creative_Commons_Attribution-ShareAlike_3.0_Unported_License>

So for now I will remain a reader in the community. I think it is worth exploring the work of this initiative!
